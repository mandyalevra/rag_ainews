# AI Daily Digest — May 14, 2026

**TL;DR:** GitHub Copilot gained auto model selection and Google launched an AI-powered collective portrait experience at the National Portrait Gallery using Gemini. · Cerebras Systems priced its IPO at $185 per share, raising $5.6B and valuing the AI chip company at nearly $40B. · Colorado's governor is set to sign a revised AI bill as state legislatures across the country flooded the zone with 50 AI law updates in a single week.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **GitHub Copilot picks its own model now.** GitHub quietly shipped auto model selection for Copilot's cloud agent, letting it choose the best available model for each task rather than relying on a user's manual pick. The Copilot CLI also expanded model support for its Rubber Duck debugging assistant. Small quality-of-life updates, but they signal a shift toward agents that manage their own tooling. [github.blog](https://github.blog/changelog/)
- **Google turns Gemini into a national art project.** Google Arts & Culture launched 'A National Portrait' at the UK's National Portrait Gallery, letting anyone 18+ submit a selfie that gets transformed into an animated charcoal-style portrait using the Gemini image model. The collective piece evolves in real time on display at the gallery through October. It's one of the more imaginative public deployments of a frontier image model to date. [blog.google](https://blog.google/company-news/outreach-and-initiatives/arts-culture/es-devlins-a-national-portrait-for-the-national-portrait-gallery/)
- **ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets goes global.** OpenAI made ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets available worldwide for ChatGPT Business, giving teams a native sidebar to build, clean, and explain spreadsheets with full app and skills support. Business customers get a free preview until June 2, after which standard plan credits apply. It's a direct play for the enterprise workflow market currently dominated by Microsoft Copilot. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/ar/articles/11391654-chatgpt-business-release-notes)
- **Kling Video 3.0 lands on Replicate.** Kling Video 3.0 is now available via Replicate, offering cinematic video generation up to 15 seconds with multi-shot control, native audio, and improved consistency. The Omni variant adds multimodal reference inputs and video editing capabilities. ByteDance's model is increasingly competitive with Sora and other frontier video generators. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/official)

## 💰 AI Startup & Investment News
- **Cerebras goes public at $40B, blows past its range.** Cerebras Systems priced its IPO at $185 per share — well above its initial $115–$125 range and a revised $150–$160 band — raising $5.6 billion in what is the largest IPO of the year so far. The AI chip maker's market cap at launch sits near $40B, a 73% jump from its Series H valuation just four months ago. Early backers including Foundation Capital and Fidelity are among the winners. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/14/cerebras-ipo)
- **Wirestock raises $23M to feed AI labs creative data.** Wirestock, a platform that sources creative multi-modal data from a community of contributors, closed a $23M Series A led by Nava Ventures, with SBVP (co-founded by Sheryl Sandberg) and others participating. The company has an annual run-rate of $40M and has paid out $15M to its contributor network. As AI labs' hunger for high-quality training data grows, Wirestock is positioning itself as a premium supplier. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/14/wirestock-raises-23m-to-supply-multi-modal-data-to-ai-labs/)
- **Nectar Social pulls $30M to run brand DMs with AI.** Nectar Social, which builds AI agents to manage brand social media accounts including direct messaging, raised a $30M Series A disclosed exclusively to Axios. The bet is that brands want personalized audience engagement at scale and will pay for AI to handle it. The round reflects growing investor appetite for vertical AI agents tackling specific marketing workflows. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/pro/all-deals/2026/05/14/nectar-social-brand-management)
- **Synthetic gets $10M from Khosla to do your startup's books.** Synthetic raised a $10M seed round led by Khosla Ventures to build an AI-native bookkeeping service aimed specifically at software startups. Founder Ian Crosby told Axios the company runs the entire bookkeeping process with AI, with minimal human intervention. It's a crowded space, but Khosla's backing gives the startup serious credibility. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/pro/fintech-deals/2026/05/14/synthetic-10m-ai-bookkeeping-seed)
- **Modal in talks to raise at $4.5B, up 80% from months ago.** Modal, which rents Nvidia GPUs and developer tooling to teams building and running AI models, is in advanced discussions to raise at a roughly $4.5B valuation — an 80% premium to its last round from just a few months prior. The surge reflects explosive demand for GPU-backed infrastructure as agent workloads scale. No close date has been announced. [theinformation.com](https://www.theinformation.com/articles)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **Colorado replaces its AI Act — and Polis will sign the swap.** Governor Jared Polis announced he will sign SB26-189, a replacement for the Colorado AI Act that had been challenged in court by xAI and the DOJ, who alleged the original law required discrimination. The new bill passed the state legislature and is intended to sidestep the legal battle while preserving consumer protections. It's a rare case of a state proactively rewriting AI law in response to a federal lawsuit. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **50 state AI law updates in one week.** Trackers logged 50 AI bill updates across 14 states this week alone, touching everything from landlord price-fixing algorithms and mental health chatbots to deepfake protections for K-12 students and AI companion regulation. The sheer volume underscores how state legislatures have become the de facto arena for AI governance while federal action stalls. Iowa's Conversational AI Safety Act and New York's annual AI hiring impact reporting requirement were among the notable moves. [ailawsbystate.com](https://www.ailawsbystate.com/press/this-week)
- **House passes financial AI sandbox bill, barely.** The House Financial Services Committee passed Chairman French Hill's bill to create regulatory sandboxes for AI tools in the financial sector, shielding companies from enforcement action while they test new products. The vote drew some Democratic support but exposed a sharp partisan rift between the committee's top leaders. The sandbox model is gaining traction as Congress struggles to pass comprehensive AI legislation. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **Pallone probes 25 companies on AI surveillance pricing.** Rep. Frank Pallone (D-NJ), ranking member of the House Energy and Commerce Committee, sent letters to 25 companies demanding details on how they use AI and machine learning alongside personal data to set consumer prices. The investigation targets so-called surveillance pricing — dynamic pricing driven by individual behavioral profiles. It's one of the more pointed congressional inquiries into AI's commercial practices this year. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **Trump's EO on state AI laws faces a legal gauntlet.** A new CSIS podcast unpacks Trump's executive order targeting state-level AI regulations, noting that while the final version is somewhat narrowed from an earlier draft, it still faces significant legal and political headwinds. Critics argue the EO overreaches federal authority by attempting to preempt state laws. The episode also examines Meta's reliance on scam-driven ad revenue as a parallel case study in the risks of light-touch tech regulation. [csis.org](https://www.csis.org/podcasts/ai-policy-podcast/trump-signs-eo-targeting-state-ai-laws-while-meta-showcases-risks-weak)
