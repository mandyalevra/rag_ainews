## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **ChatGPT moves into your spreadsheets.** OpenAI's ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets is now available globally across all plan tiers, living as a sidebar inside your spreadsheets. It can build, update, explain, and summarize workbooks using natural language — including large multi-tab files with complex formulas. Business and Enterprise customers get a free preview through June 2, after which credits and usage terms apply. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/id-id/articles/20001063-chatgpt-for-excel-and-google-sheets)
- **FLUX 2 lands on Replicate with character consistency.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX 2 Max is now available via Replicate's API, supporting up to 8 reference images to maintain character consistency across image batches. The model targets product photography, cinematic frames, and precise color control. A lighter 4B variant, FLUX 2 Klein, supports up to 5 references for faster generation. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)
- **Hugging Face ships a linter for Transformers model files.** Hugging Face quietly released mlinter, a linting tool designed specifically for Transformers modeling files. The tool aims to catch common errors and enforce consistency in model code before they ship. It's a small but telling sign that AI model development tooling is maturing toward software engineering best practices. [huggingface.co](https://huggingface.co/huggingface)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **White House blinks on mandatory AI model vetting.** After NEC Director Kevin Hassett floated the idea of FDA-style mandatory reviews for frontier AI models, Chief of Staff Susie Wiles quickly walked it back, posting that the administration favors empowering innovators over bureaucracy. Tech lobbyists say the reversal was welcome but left them more confused about the White House's actual plan. The episode exposed real disorganization at the top of AI policy inside the Trump administration. [politico.com](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/05/08/trump-white-house-ai-confusion-00913092)
- **Big AI labs sign voluntary testing deal with federal government.** Google, Microsoft, xAI, Anthropic, and OpenAI have all agreed to pre-release model testing through CAISI, NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation, covering cyber, biological, and chemical risk evaluations. The deal is the Trump administration's preferred alternative to binding regulation, keeping oversight voluntary and industry-led. Advocates say it's a meaningful first step; critics argue it lacks teeth for high-risk systems. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **Florida moves on AI 'bill of rights' targeting chatbots and minors.** A special session of the Florida legislature has begun considering an AI bill of rights championed by Governor Ron DeSantis, focused on creating guardrails around chatbots to protect children. Industry groups and the Trump White House oppose the measure, arguing it conflicts with the federal push for uniform national standards. The bill puts Florida in the same reform camp as Colorado, which has its own AI law taking effect June 30. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **DOJ backs xAI's lawsuit against Colorado's AI Act.** The Department of Justice has filed a motion to intervene in xAI's lawsuit against the Colorado attorney general, supporting the company's argument that the state's AI non-discrimination law is unconstitutional. The move marks a significant escalation of the federal government's posture against state-level AI regulation. Colorado's law is scheduled to take effect on June 30, 2026. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **30 nations gather in Seoul to propose global frontier model registry.** Representatives from 30 countries met at the 2026 AI Safety Summit in Seoul to discuss an international registry for frontier AI models, requiring developers above a certain compute threshold to disclose training data and internal safety evaluations. The proposal aims to prevent any country or lab from building a potentially dangerous system entirely without accountability. A parallel consortium also proposed a universal watermarking standard for AI-generated content to combat voice cloning scams. [youtube.com](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cefiN7Kxb0I)
