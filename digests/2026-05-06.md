# AI Daily Digest — May 6, 2026

**TL;DR:** OpenAI quietly rolls out GPT-5.5 to paid users today, claiming it cuts hallucinations by more than half while xAI prepares to retire a wave of legacy Grok models on May 15. · Finnish AI lab QuTwo hits a $380M valuation on a lean €25M angel round backed by Yuri Milner and Thomas Wolf, while agentic payments startup Natural closes in on $30M at a $150M valuation. · The Trump administration reversed its hands-off stance, striking pre-deployment review deals with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI after Anthropic's unreleased Mythos model spooked national security officials.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **GPT-5.5 ships to paid ChatGPT users.** OpenAI began rolling out GPT-5.5 today for Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise subscribers in ChatGPT and Codex. The new Thinking tier claims to cut hallucinations by over half and handles multi-step tasks — spreadsheets, frontend code, advanced math — far better than its predecessors. API access is coming soon but isn't live yet, as OpenAI says it requires additional safety work at scale. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/fr-ca/articles/11909943-gpt-55-in-chatgpt)
- **xAI retiring eight Grok models on May 15.** xAI's API docs confirm that eight models — including grok-4-fast variants, grok-3, grok-code-fast-1, and grok-imagine-image-pro — will be switched off on May 15 at noon PT. Developers are being pushed toward the newer Grok 4.3 lineup, which xAI says excels at agentic reasoning and tool use. A migration guide is live now. [docs.x.ai](https://docs.x.ai/developers/models)
- **FLUX 2 brings sub-second image gen to production apps.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX 2 family is now fully available on Replicate, headlined by FLUX.2 Klein 4B — a 4B-parameter distilled model that generates images in under a second, suited for real-time applications and consumer hardware. The flagship FLUX.2 Max supports up to eight reference images for character consistency and precise hex-level color control. Replicate recommends FLUX.2 Pro as the practical default for most production use cases. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)
- **Google pumps $10M more into Asia-Pacific AI education.** Google expanded its AI Opportunity Fund for Asia-Pacific today with an additional $10 million, bringing total regional commitment to $37 million. The program targets 4.7 million learners and educators across 19 countries, including Indonesia, with structured AI learning pathways and a new AI Playbook for Educators. It partners with over 20 local organizations to align training with national digital agendas. [blog.google](https://blog.google/intl/id-id/company-news/outreach-initiatives/ai-opportunity-fund-di-apac-2026/)

## 💰 AI Startup & Investment News
- **QuTwo valued at $380M on a scrappy angel round.** Finnish AI lab QuTwo, founded by former AMD Silo AI CEO Peter Sarlin, raised a €25M angel round at a €325M ($380M) post-money valuation. Backers include Yuri Milner, Xavier Niel, Supercell's Ilkka Paananen, and Hugging Face's Thomas Wolf. The company already has $23M in committed revenue from design partnerships including Zalando, and Sarlin says the modest round size is intentional — less pressure, more focus. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/05/peter-sarlins-qutwo-reaches-380m-valuation-in-angel-round/)
- **Natural raises $30M for AI-native payments.** Natural, a startup building payment infrastructure for AI agents, is closing a $30M Series A at a $150M post-money valuation, per Axios Pro. The raise signals growing investor conviction that autonomous agents will need their own financial rails — not just API keys. Terms were still being finalized at time of reporting. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/pro/fintech-deals/2026/05/05/natural-ai-agent-payments-series-a)
- **Bessemer backs AI architecture platform Illoca at seed.** Illoca, which brings AI-assisted design tools to architects, raised $13M in seed funding led by Bessemer Venture Partners. The raise is an early signal that AEC (architecture, engineering, construction) software is becoming a serious vertical for AI investment. Details on the product roadmap were not disclosed. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/pro/enterprise-software-deals/2026/05/06/illoca-ai-architecture-13m-raise)
- **Public buys Treasury App in AI investing push.** Brokerage platform Public acquired Treasury App, an investment education startup, in a move to deepen its AI-powered financial guidance offering. The deal was confirmed exclusively to Axios by Public COO Stephen Sikes, though financial terms were not disclosed. Public has been positioning itself as an AI-first retail investing platform. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/pro/fintech-deals/2026/05/06/public-treasury-app-ai-investing-acquisition)
- **CellCentric banks $220M for myeloma drug, eyes IPO.** Biotech firm CellCentric closed a $220M Series D led by Venrock, with co-investors including Pfizer and the American Cancer Society's BrightEdge fund, to advance its multiple myeloma treatment into late-stage trials. The company is now approaching a $1B valuation and has enough runway through 2029. CEO Will West told Axios a potential IPO is under consideration for the coming year. [axios.com](https://www.axios.com/pro/biotech-deals/2026/05/06/cellcentric-raises-220m-myeloma-drug)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **Trump's government will preview AI models before launch.** The US government announced Tuesday that it has secured pre-deployment review agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI — a sharp U-turn from the Trump administration's earlier hands-off posture. The Commerce Department's CAISI will conduct evaluations of frontier models before public release. The direct catalyst, officials and reporters confirm, was Anthropic's Mythos model, which the NSA is now testing after concerns it can autonomously exploit zero-day software vulnerabilities. [canadianaffairs.news](https://www.canadianaffairs.news/2026/05/06/u-s-to-assess-new-ai-models-before-their-release/)
- **White House drafts executive order for AI review board.** Politico reports the White House is drafting a 16-page executive order that would create a working group of tech executives and government officials to review frontier AI models, address cybersecurity risks from open-weight models, and potentially involve the intelligence community. The apparent reversal is alarming some laissez-faire Silicon Valley voices who worry it will slow innovation relative to China. The draft remains in flux. [politico.com](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/05/05/white-house-mulls-tight-new-controls-on-advanced-ai-00907468)
- **Connecticut passes landmark AI workplace transparency law.** Connecticut's legislature passed the AI Responsibility and Transparency Act 131-17 in the House and 32-4 in the Senate, and Governor Lamont confirmed he will sign it. The law requires employers to disclose when AI is used in hiring, promotion, or termination decisions, and crucially mandates that any federal WARN-notice layoff disclose whether AI drove the job cuts — a provision taking effect October 1, 2026. It directly amends anti-discrimination law to prevent AI tools from serving as a legal shield against bias claims. [futureforwarded.substack.com](https://futureforwarded.substack.com/p/the-ai-labor-report-wednesday-may)
- **Federal court freezes Colorado's AI Act weeks before deadline.** A federal court paused enforcement of Colorado's sweeping AI Act on April 27, just weeks before its June 30 effective date, after a leading AI developer filed a constitutional challenge and the DOJ intervened — marking the federal government's first litigation move against a state AI law. Colorado's legislature is now racing to amend or replace the statute before its session ends May 13. Even with enforcement frozen, legal experts warn employers still face liability under existing anti-discrimination and privacy laws. [theemployerreport.com](https://www.theemployerreport.com/2026/05/ai-regulation-on-hold-in-colorado-but-employer-risk-isnt/)
- **Musk vs. Altman trial continues to heat up.** The courtroom battle between Elon Musk and Sam Altman over OpenAI's mission and governance continued Wednesday, with Musk's legal team arguing the company abandoned its nonprofit mandate in pursuit of profit. Altman's side contends the commercial evolution was necessary to compete at the frontier. Legal observers say the outcome could set major precedents for how AI companies are structured and governed globally. [youtube.com](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NQxt37oVSw)
