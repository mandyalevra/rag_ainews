## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **Gemini remembers you — and will steal your ChatGPT history.** Google is rolling out a Memories feature to UK Gemini users that lets the assistant learn preferences across conversations over time. More aggressively, it now lets users import their full chat history and saved memories from rival AI apps via ZIP file or a copy-paste prompt flow. The move is a direct shot at user lock-in across the AI assistant market. [blog.google](https://blog.google/company-news/inside-google/around-the-globe/google-europe/united-kingdom/gemini-launches-new-personalisation-features-in-the-uk/)
- **Kiri.ai drops a 1-trillion-parameter open model on Replicate.** Moonshot AI's Kimi model — 1 trillion parameters, 262k context window, with vision and tool use — is now available via Replicate's API alongside a wave of other frontier models. The Replicate catalog now spans everything from OpenAI's GPT image generation to Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 and ByteDance's multimodal video model. The breadth signals that production-ready model access is becoming a commodity. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/explore)
- **FLUX 2 Max targets product photography and character consistency.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX 2 family now covers the full spectrum from sub-second prototyping with FLUX 2 Klein to premium fidelity with FLUX 2 Max, which supports up to 8 reference images for character and product consistency. Structured JSON prompting and precise hex color control make it a serious contender for commercial creative workflows. The family is available production-ready via Replicate. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)
- **Google Translate turns 20 with Gemini-powered idiom smarts.** Google marked the 20th anniversary of Translate by integrating Gemini models into its Live interpretation feature, which now handles slang, idioms, and tonal nuance rather than just literal translation. Earbuds can now act as real-time interpreters that preserve a speaker's rhythm and register. The upgrade reflects how Gemini is quietly becoming the engine behind Google's entire product surface. [blog.google](https://blog.google/intl/zh-tw/products/explore-get-answers/google-translate-20/)
- **FIDO Alliance sets safety rules for AI agents buying things online.** The FIDO Alliance announced new authentication standards specifically designed for AI agents that shop, log in, and transact on users' behalf. The initiative addresses a fast-growing identity problem: as agents proliferate, most organizations have no controls over which agents access which data or accounts. The standards aim to prevent agents from going rogue with payment credentials before the problem becomes widespread. [aiagentstore.ai](https://aiagentstore.ai/ai-agent-news/2026-april)

It previously raised a $15M seed in January 2025, making this a rapid scale-up in a sector attracting serious venture capital. The round comes weeks after rival RadAI closed a $60M Series C at a $525M valuation, signaling fierce competition in radiology AI. ## 🌐 General AI News
- **EU AI Act talks collapse after marathon session.** After 12 hours of negotiations, EU member states and European Parliament lawmakers failed to agree on amendments to the AI Act that would have delayed high-risk compliance deadlines from August 2026 to 2027 or later. The core sticking point is whether AI embedded in already-regulated products like medical devices and toys should be exempt from additional AI Act requirements. If talks stall through May and June, companies that built their timelines around the extension could face immediate compliance obligations they are not ready for. [thenextweb.com](https://thenextweb.com/news/eu-ai-act-omnibus-deal-fails-april-2026-talks)
- **Google signed a classified AI deal with the Pentagon — quietly.** Google reportedly agreed to let the US Department of Defense use its AI models for any lawful government purpose under a classified contract, placing it alongside OpenAI and xAI in the set of companies with secret military AI agreements. The deal reportedly bars the company from blocking lawful government uses and requires it to help adjust safety filters when asked. The contract was finalized less than a day after Google employees publicly asked Sundar Pichai to block Pentagon access. [youtube.com](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ub6yRNolFgg)
- **Musk takes the stand in his OpenAI trial.** Elon Musk began testifying in his lawsuit against OpenAI, Sam Altman, and Greg Brockman in a California federal courtroom. Musk is alleging fraud, breach of charitable trust, and unjust enrichment, and is seeking to unwind OpenAI's for-profit restructuring and strip Altman and Brockman of authority. The trial is expected to call major industry witnesses including Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati. [youtube.com](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ub6yRNolFgg)
- **AI regulation is fracturing into sovereign bubbles.** A new analysis argues that the long-anticipated convergence of global AI rules is not happening — instead, 2026 is seeing a mosaic of incompatible national regimes. Australia is advancing workplace AI law, Pakistan is restricting government hiring AI, US states are passing a flurry of bills while the White House pushes federal preemption, and the EU can't even agree with itself. For multinationals, the compliance burden is becoming a competitive disadvantage, and shadow AI use is growing as strict internal policies push employees toward personal accounts. [questa-ai.com](https://www.questa-ai.com/privacy-cafe/ai-regulation-in-2026-is-breaking-apart-globally)
- **The US should build AI standards, not bully AI companies.** A sharp analysis argues that the Trump administration's confrontation with Anthropic — threatening to blacklist the company for refusing to remove safety guardrails from Pentagon access — is the wrong play, and risks damaging US credibility in international AI governance. The piece draws a historical parallel to how the US shaped the internet by leading protocol standards through DARPA and NIST, and argues that CAISI's new AI Agent Standards Initiative is the right model to follow. Countries considering adopting US-led AI infrastructure are watching the Anthropic episode carefully. [techpolicy.press](https://www.techpolicy.press/the-us-is-fighting-for-control-of-ai-it-would-be-better-off-building-standards/)
