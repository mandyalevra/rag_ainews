# AI Daily Digest — May 3, 2026

**TL;DR:** OpenAI's new ChatGPT Go tier and a flurry of GPT-5.x model updates are reshaping how different user segments access reasoning and multimodal features. · Anthropic is racing toward a $50B raise at a near-$900B valuation, while DeepMind alum David Silver's brand-new lab pulled in $1.1B in its first months. · Connecticut became the third state to pass a comprehensive AI bill this year as Florida's special session collapsed and Colorado moved to gut its own landmark AI Act.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **ChatGPT Go launches worldwide.** OpenAI rolled out ChatGPT Go globally — a budget tier sitting between Free and Plus that bundles unlimited GPT-5.3 Instant chats, extended image generation, file uploads, and a larger memory window. The tier is explicitly designed to pull in price-sensitive users, and OpenAI has flagged it may eventually carry ads. Enterprise customers retain the option to set Go-grade models as their default routing choice. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/zh-hant-hk/articles/11989085-%E7%94%9A%E9%BA%BC%E6%98%AF-chatgpt-go)
- **GPT-5.4 Thinking lands, mini fallback quietly added.** OpenAI's model changelog shows GPT-5.4 Thinking shipped in early March as the single flagship combining reasoning, coding, and agentic workflows. The companion GPT-5.4 mini was introduced in mid-March as an automatic fallback when rate limits are hit, and will not appear as a selectable model option within 30 days. The previous GPT-5 Thinking mini is being deprecated on the same timeline. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/th-th/articles/9624314-model-release-notes)
- **FLUX 2 family brings sub-second generation and 10-image blending.** Black Forest Labs' second-generation FLUX lineup is now available via Replicate, spanning Max (highest fidelity, hex-color control, 8-reference character consistency), Pro (structured JSON prompting, multi-reference editing), and Klein-4B (sub-second generation from a 4B-parameter distilled model). Editing tools including Fill, Depth, and Canny round out the suite for inpainting, retexturing, and style transfer. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)
- **NVIDIA releases Nemotron agentic datasets on Hugging Face.** NVIDIA published a new collection on Hugging Face under the Nemotron Agentic & Tool-Use banner, providing datasets specifically built for function calling, multi-step agent tasks, terminal use, and software-engineering workflows. Separately, NVIDIA also announced the Ising model family aimed at using AI to advance practical quantum computing. [huggingface.co](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/collections)
- **Google Gemini becomes Kevin Pietersen's cricket co-creator.** Google India announced a partnership with cricket icon Kevin Pietersen in which Gemini serves as the creative backbone of his new YouTube series The Switch — generating player cards, composing match anthems, building quizzes, and distilling complex stats for global audiences. The series drops twice weekly starting May 4, featuring an extensive lineup of international cricket stars. [blog.google](https://blog.google/intl/en-in/products/the-new-opening-partnership-how-kevin-pietersen-google-are-changing-the-game/)

## 💰 AI Startup & Investment News
- **Anthropic may close $50B round at $900B valuation within days.** Anthropic has asked investors to submit allocations within 48 hours for what could be a $50B fundraise, with pre-emptive offers landing in the $850B–$900B valuation range, according to TechCrunch sources. If it closes at the top of that range it would be among the largest private company fundraises in history, making Anthropic worth roughly as much as Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley combined. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **David Silver's Ineffable Intelligence raises $1.1B on day one.** Ineffable Intelligence, a British AI lab founded just months ago by DeepMind legend David Silver — the researcher behind AlphaGo — has secured $1.1B in funding at a $5.1B valuation. The lab's thesis is that AI should be able to learn powerful representations without relying on human-labeled data, a direct challenge to the dominant RLHF paradigm. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **137 Ventures raises $700M to back SpaceX, Anduril-style growth bets.** SpaceX-backer 137 Ventures closed over $700M across two new growth-stage funds, doubling down on the defense-tech and deep-tech companies that defined its first vintage. The firm's portfolio includes SpaceX, Anduril, and Hadrian, and the new capital will target similar companies at later stages before IPO. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **BMW i Ventures launches $300M fund for agentic and physical AI.** BMW i Ventures unveiled its third fund at $300M, bringing total capital under management to $1.1B and signaling the auto industry's conviction that agentic AI will reshape manufacturing and supply chains. The fund targets early-stage through Series B startups in North America and Europe working on physical AI, advanced materials, and industrial software. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/03/techcrunch-mobility-how-do-you-issue-a-ticket-to-a-robotaxi/)
- **Skio sells for $105M after raising just $8M — a rare clean exit.** Y Combinator alum Skio, a subscription commerce platform, was acquired for $105M in an all-cash deal despite having raised only $8M in outside funding, making it one of the more capital-efficient exits in recent memory. The founder confirmed the sale price publicly, offering a counterpoint to the mega-round frenzy dominating AI headlines this week. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **Connecticut passes sweeping AI bill; Florida's collapses.** Connecticut's SB 5 — covering frontier models, chatbots, employment decisions, and content provenance — cleared the legislature and Governor Lamont says he will sign it, marking the state's third attempt after two prior vetoes. Meanwhile Florida's special session ended in failure: the Senate passed an AI Bill of Rights in hours, but the House killed it immediately, leaving Governor DeSantis' signature AI initiative dead for this session. [troutmanprivacy.com](https://www.troutmanprivacy.com/2026/05/proposed-state-ai-law-update-may-4-2026/)
- **Colorado moves to gut its own AI Act.** Senate Majority Leader Robert Rodriguez introduced SB 189, a bill to repeal and replace Colorado's landmark AI Act with a disclosure-only regime, stripping away the algorithmic-impact assessments and high-risk-use restrictions that made the original law controversial. The bill is the product of Governor Polis' own workgroup and must pass before the legislature closes May 13. [troutmanprivacy.com](https://www.troutmanprivacy.com/2026/05/proposed-state-ai-law-update-may-4-2026/)
- **DOJ joins xAI in fighting Colorado's existing AI Act in court.** The Department of Justice filed a motion to intervene in xAI's federal lawsuit challenging the Colorado AI Act, arguing the law violates the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. The move signals that the Trump administration is willing to deploy federal legal resources to block state-level AI regulation, a precedent with significant implications for the patchwork of bills advancing in over a dozen states. [insideaipolicy.com](https://insideaipolicy.com)
- **Musk's $130B OpenAI lawsuit hits day one in court.** Elon Musk took the stand in his $130B lawsuit against OpenAI, arguing that CEO Sam Altman effectively converted a charitable organization for private gain and that allowing it would undermine American philanthropic structures. OpenAI's defense framed the suit as sour grapes from a founder who left and watched the company succeed, with hundreds of pages of private messages set to become public over the coming month. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **Google signs classified Pentagon AI deal despite 600-employee revolt.** Google finalized a classified AI contract with the Pentagon the same week more than 600 employees published an open letter demanding leadership reject military AI workloads. The contract reportedly grants the Department of Defense access to Google's AI models for any lawful government purpose, with no veto rights reserved for Google — a definitive reversal of the company's 2018 no-weapons pledge, which was removed from its AI principles earlier this year. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
