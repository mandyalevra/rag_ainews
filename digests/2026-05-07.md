# AI Daily Digest — May 7, 2026

**TL;DR:** OpenAI rolled out GPT-5.5 to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users in ChatGPT and Codex, while xAI announced it will retire several Grok 4 model variants on May 15. · China's Moonshot AI raised $2B at a $20B valuation as demand for open-source frontier models surges, and DeepSeek eyes a $45B valuation from its first-ever investment round. · The White House is drafting an executive order that could require government pre-approval of powerful AI models before public release, sparking fierce industry backlash.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **GPT-5.5 lands for paying ChatGPT users.** OpenAI launched GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro today for Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise subscribers inside ChatGPT and Codex, with a 256K context window on the Thinking variant. The rollout is gradual and API access is coming soon, pending additional safety measures for scale. Enterprise workspaces can also redirect Auto routing to the cheaper Thinking mini to save credits. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/es-419/articles/11909943-gpt-55-in-chatgpt)
- **ChatGPT moves into your spreadsheets.** OpenAI's ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets is now available globally to Free, Go, Plus, Pro, Business, Enterprise, Edu, and K-12 users as a sidebar add-in. It can build, update, and explain multi-tab spreadsheets with complex formulas using natural language. Business and Enterprise customers get a free preview through June 2, 2026, after which credits and usage terms apply. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/ru-ru/articles/20001063-chatgpt-for-excel-and-google-sheets-%D0%B2-%D0%B1%D0%B5%D1%82%D0%B0-%D0%B2%D0%B5%D1%80%D1%81%D0%B8%D0%B8)
- **xAI sunsets Grok 4 fast models on May 15.** xAI announced it will retire eight Grok models — including grok-4-1-fast-reasoning, grok-4-fast-non-reasoning, grok-code-fast-1, and grok-imagine-image-pro — effective May 15 at noon PT. Any API requests to these endpoints after that date will fail, and developers are urged to migrate now. xAI hinted at exciting new model releases in the coming weeks. [docs.x.ai](https://docs.x.ai/developers/migration/may-15-retirement)
- **FLUX 2 brings sub-second image gen to production apps.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX 2 model family — including Max, Pro, Flex, and the 4B Klein variant — is now available via Replicate's API with structured JSON prompting, hex color control, and support for up to 10 reference images. The Klein 4B model generates images in under one second, making it viable for real-time applications. FLUX.2 Pro handles both generation and editing at a lower price point than Max. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)

## 💰 AI Startup & Investment News
- **Moonshot AI raises $2B at a $20B valuation.** China's Moonshot AI closed a $2 billion funding round valuing the company at $20 billion, riding surging demand for open-source frontier models. The company's flagship Kimi model features a 1-trillion-parameter architecture, a 262K context window, and capabilities spanning vision, tool use, and autonomous software engineering. The raise signals that Chinese AI labs are increasingly competitive with their U.S. counterparts on both capability and capital. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **DeepSeek eyes $45B valuation in debut funding round.** DeepSeek, the Chinese AI lab that stunned the industry in early 2025 with its highly efficient models, is in discussions for its first-ever investment round at a potential $45 billion valuation. The lab rose to prominence by training frontier-level models at a fraction of the compute cost of U.S. competitors like OpenAI and Google. A deal at that valuation would make it one of the most valuable AI startups globally without a prior institutional round. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **Quantum Motion raises $160M Series C.** UK-based quantum computing startup Quantum Motion closed a $160 million Series C co-led by DCVC and Mundi Ventures, one of the largest European quantum fundraises to date. The round included the British Business Bank, Firgun Ventures, Oxford Science Enterprises, and strategic investors Bosch Ventures and Porsche. The raise comes as European quantum startups secured €1.6B in 2025, more than doubling the prior year's total. [sifted.eu](https://sifted.eu/articles/quantum-motion-series-c)
- **SAP pays $1.16B for 18-month-old German AI lab.** SAP is acquiring Prior Labs, a German AI startup, in a deal valued at $1.16 billion — extraordinary for a company less than two years old. The acquisition signals SAP's aggressive push to embed advanced AI into its enterprise software stack. SAP also announced it is restricting its customers' AI agents to a select list of approved providers, including Nvidia's NemoClaw. [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)
- **a16z leads $16M seed in Stockholm AI startup Pit.** Pit, a Stockholm-based AI-native startup co-founded by the team behind scooter company Voi, raised a $16 million seed round led by Andreessen Horowitz with participation from Lakestar and angels from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Deel, and Revolut. The round also saw backing from Swedish industrial families Stena and Lundin. Details on Pit's product remain sparse, but the caliber of backers signals high expectations. [sifted.eu](https://sifted.eu/articles/a16z-leads-16m-seed-in-voi-cofounders-new-ai-startup-pit)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **White House eyes pre-release AI model approval.** The Trump administration is drafting an executive order that could require government testing and sign-off before powerful AI models are released to the public, with one official comparing it to FDA drug approval. The proposal emerged after meetings about Anthropic's Mythos model and its potential cybersecurity implications. Free-market groups and industry advisers are pushing back hard, warning that any veto power over model releases could violate First Amendment principles. [politico.com](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/digital-future-daily/2026/05/06/why-ai-vetting-is-freaking-people-out-00908708)
- **DOJ sides with Colorado against xAI in landmark AI lawsuit.** The U.S. Department of Justice intervened in xAI's lawsuit challenging Colorado's high-risk AI law, arguing the legislation violates the 14th Amendment's equal protection clause. xAI has separately raised First Amendment concerns about the law, which takes effect June 30 and requires developers to guard against discriminatory outcomes in employment, housing, healthcare, and financial services. The DOJ's entry into the case significantly raises the legal stakes for state-level AI regulation nationwide. [alisarmustafa.substack.com](https://alisarmustafa.substack.com/p/the-ai-policy-newsletter-05062026)
- **House introduces sweeping bipartisan AI bill.** Representatives Ted Lieu and Jay Obernolte introduced the American Leadership in Artificial Intelligence Act, a package combining more than 20 bipartisan proposals drawn from the 2024 AI Task Force report. The bill covers AI standards through NIST, federal procurement reform, workforce development, and safeguards against AI-enabled crime and synthetic media. Connecticut also passed its own comprehensive AI bill this week, covering employment decisions, youth protections, and a regulatory sandbox. [alisarmustafa.substack.com](https://alisarmustafa.substack.com/p/the-ai-policy-newsletter-05062026)
- **South Africa pulls AI policy over fake citations.** South Africa's government withdrew its draft national AI policy after officials discovered the document contained fictitious references that appeared to be AI-generated. Communications Minister Solly Malatsi confirmed the sources were included without proper verification, an embarrassing episode for a policy meant to govern AI use. The draft had proposed creating a National AI Commission and an AI Regulatory Authority; no revised timeline has been announced. [alisarmustafa.substack.com](https://alisarmustafa.substack.com/p/the-ai-policy-newsletter-05062026)
- **Chinese court: firing workers for AI replacement is illegal.** The Hangzhou Intermediate People's Court ruled that employers cannot terminate workers solely because AI has automated their role, ordering compensation for a quality-assurance employee who was fired after his LLM-output review job was automated away. The employer had offered a demotion with a pay cut, which the worker refused before being let go under a staffing-reduction rationale. The ruling sets a significant precedent as AI-driven workforce displacement accelerates across China.
