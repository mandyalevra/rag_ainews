## 🌐 General AI News
- **Judge blocks Pentagon's Anthropic ban as First Amendment retaliation.** A federal judge ruled that the Department of Defense's supply chain risk designation against Anthropic — which effectively barred the company from federal contracts — was unconstitutional retaliation for the company's public criticism, not a legitimate security threat. The ruling is a significant win for Anthropic and raises questions about how agencies can use procurement rules to pressure AI developers. The White House separately confirmed it is drafting plans to permit federal use of Anthropic's models. [nextgov.com](https://www.nextgov.com/topic/artificial-intelligence/)
- **GSA draft clause could hand government ownership of AI contractors' IP.** The General Services Administration has circulated a draft procurement clause that would transfer ownership of any custom AI development — including fine-tuned models, configurations, and enhancements — from vendors to the federal government. The U.S. Chamber of Commerce and other industry groups warn the language is so broad it could cover embedded AI in standard commercial software, chill competition, and deter participation in federal contracts. Businesses are pushing the GSA to narrow definitions and explicitly preserve pre-existing IP rights. [feda.com](https://www.feda.com/news/draft-federal-ai-contract-rules-could-shift-intellectual-property-rights-and-place-reporting-burdens)
- **Microsoft, Google, and xAI agree to pre-launch government model reviews.** Three of the biggest AI players — Microsoft, Google, and Elon Musk's xAI — have agreed to submit new AI models to the Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation for pre-deployment safety evaluations before public release. The agreement, which follows alarm over Anthropic's Claude Mythos autonomously discovering zero-day exploits, marks a significant shift toward structured federal oversight of frontier AI. Critics are watching closely to see whether the process accelerates responsible deployment or simply adds friction. [nextgov.com](https://www.nextgov.com/topic/artificial-intelligence/)
- **Trump EO takes aim at state AI laws as Florida debates AI bill of rights.** President Trump signed an executive order targeting state-level AI legislation, attempting to preempt a patchwork of rules with federal standards — a move that faces immediate legal and political challenges. Simultaneously, Florida's legislature opened a special session on Gov. DeSantis's proposed AI bill of rights, focused on protecting minors from chatbots, which the Trump White House and industry groups both oppose. The divergence highlights a widening fault line between state ambitions and federal deregulatory instincts. [csis.org](https://www.csis.org/podcasts/ai-policy-podcast/trump-signs-eo-targeting-state-ai-laws-while-meta-showcases-risks-weak)
