# AI Daily Digest — May 08, 2026

**TL;DR:** GitHub Copilot is deprecating GPT-4.1 and rolling out a wave of new security and enterprise features throughout May. · DeepSeek is chasing a $7.35B funding round while a six-week-old ex-OpenAI startup targets a $4B valuation. · Google, Microsoft, and xAI signed deals with the US government's CAISI to undergo pre-deployment AI safety testing — a quiet but significant shift from Trump's deregulation stance.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **ChatGPT moves into your spreadsheets.** OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets, a sidebar-native AI that can build, edit, and explain complex multi-tab spreadsheets in plain language. It's available to all plan tiers globally, with Free and Go users getting limited access. Business, Enterprise, and Edu customers get a free preview through June 2, 2026, after which usage follows their plan credits. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/it-it/articles/20001063-chatgpt-for-excel-and-google-sheets-in-beta)
- **GitHub Copilot deprecates GPT-4.1, ships security upgrades.** GitHub's May changelog is packed: GPT-4.1 is being retired on June 1 across all Copilot experiences, and GPT-5.2 and GPT-5.2-Codex are also on the chopping block. On the upside, secret scanning via the GitHub MCP Server is now generally available, dependency scanning is in public preview, and the Rubber Duck CLI review agent now supports cross-model critic pairing. [github.blog](https://github.blog/changelog/month/05-2026/)
- **FLUX 2 brings character consistency to image generation APIs.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX 2 models are now available via Replicate's API, with the flagship flux-2-max accepting up to 8 reference images for consistent character generation across batches. The family supports precise hex-color control, cinematic keyframes, and 3D view generation. A lighter 4-billion-parameter variant, flux-2-klein-4b, supports up to 5 references for faster turnaround. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)
- **OpenAI hands GPT-5.5-Cyber to the US government before launch.** OpenAI gave CAISI early access to ChatGPT 5.5 ahead of its public release for national security testing, and is co-developing a responsible deployment strategy for GPT-5.5-Cyber — a specialized model aimed at strengthening cyber defense. The model is currently limited to a first-access group while a distribution playbook for government use is being finalized. [euronews.com](https://www.euronews.com/next/2026/05/08/tech-giants-agree-to-us-government-ai-testing)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **Google, Microsoft, and xAI agree to pre-launch government AI testing.** All three companies signed new agreements with the Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) to submit their models for safety evaluations covering cybersecurity, biosecurity, and chemical weapons risks before public release. The deals represent a notable pivot for the Trump administration, which has otherwise championed deregulation. The Pentagon confirmed it will 'never again' rely on a single AI provider, framing the agreements as a counterstatement to its ongoing dispute with Anthropic. [euronews.com](https://www.euronews.com/next/2026/05/08/tech-giants-agree-to-us-government-ai-testing)
- **White House backs away from mandatory AI pre-release vetting.** After reports of a potential executive order requiring government review of frontier AI models before launch, the White House clarified it favors voluntary industry partnerships over mandatory oversight — citing concerns that approval gates could hand market advantages to whichever lab clears the process first. Behind the scenes, officials are still discussing whether the intelligence community should pre-assess powerful models to exploit capabilities before adversaries do. [politico.com](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/05/07/white-house-ai-oversight-00910837)
- **EU strikes provisional deal to water down AI Act timelines.** EU lawmakers and member states reached a provisional agreement to push back key AI Act deadlines: standalone high-risk AI systems now face a December 2027 compliance date, and AI embedded in regulated products gets until August 2028. The deal also extends simplification measures to small mid-caps and reinforces the AI Office's enforcement powers. The text still needs formal endorsement from both the Council and Parliament. [dig.watch](https://dig.watch/newsletters/dw-weekly/dw-weekly-261)
- **US and China weigh formal AI governance talks.** Washington and Beijing are considering launching official discussions on AI governance and risk — including concerns about erratic model behavior and autonomous weapons — potentially at an upcoming summit. The move would mark a rare diplomatic thaw in the tech rivalry between the two countries and signal that both sides recognize some coordination on frontier AI may be unavoidable. [politico.com](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/05/07/white-house-ai-oversight-00910837)
- **Take It Down Act goes into effect May 19 — deepfakes included.** The federal Take It Down Act (TiDA) takes effect on May 19, 2026, making it illegal to knowingly publish or threaten to publish non-consensual intimate images — including AI-generated deepfakes. Covered platforms will be required to remove such content within 48 hours of a victim's notice. It's the first major federal law directly targeting AI-generated sexual imagery. [jdsupra.com](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/recent-ai-regulatory-developments-in-6731444/)
