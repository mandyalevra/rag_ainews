**TL;DR:** Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI heads to trial as jury selection begins, while the DOJ makes an unprecedented move to invalidate Colorado's sweeping AI Act — setting up a landmark week for AI governance.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **GitHub Copilot adds Claude and Codex to Agent HQ.** GitHub's Agent HQ now lets developers pick between Anthropic's Claude and OpenAI's Codex directly inside GitHub and VS Code. The feature is in public preview for Copilot Pro+ and Enterprise subscribers, expanding the platform's multi-agent orchestration ambitions first unveiled at Universe 2025. [github.blog](https://github.blog/news-insights/company-news/)
- **GitHub Copilot SDK opens to all developers.** A new GitHub Copilot SDK is now in technical preview, letting any application embed a programmable agent layer that can plan tasks, invoke tools, edit files, and run commands. GitHub is positioning this as a way to bake AI-native workflows into third-party apps, not just its own surfaces. [github.blog](https://github.blog/news-insights/company-news/)
- **OpenAI restructures ChatGPT Pro into two tiers.** OpenAI has split its $200 ChatGPT Pro plan, adding a new $100 mid-tier that offers 5x usage limits over Plus and 10x Codex access through May 31. The restructure reflects growing demand for Codex and Deep Research among professional users who don't need the full unlimited-tier firepower. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/id-id/articles/9793128-about-chatgpt-pro-plans)
- **Google donates Agent Payments Protocol to FIDO Alliance.** Google open-sourced version 0.2 of its Agent Payments Protocol (AP2) on GitHub and handed stewardship to the FIDO Alliance. The update introduces 'Human Not Present' payment flows, allowing AI agents to autonomously complete pre-authorized purchases — like snagging concert tickets the instant they go on sale. [blog.google](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/platforms/google-pay/agent-payments-protocol-fido-alliance/)
- **FLUX.2 family brings sub-second image gen to production.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX.2 lineup on Replicate now spans three tiers: Max for ultra-high-fidelity product photography, Pro for high-volume editing with structured JSON prompting, and Klein 4B — a 4-billion-parameter distilled model capable of sub-second generation for real-time apps. All tiers support multi-reference image blending. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **Musk vs. Altman trial kicks off in California.** Jury selection began Monday in San Francisco for Elon Musk's lawsuit against Sam Altman and OpenAI, with opening arguments set for Tuesday. Musk is seeking up to $150 billion in damages, alleging OpenAI betrayed its nonprofit charter when it pivoted toward a for-profit structure — a case that could redefine how AI companies are legally obligated to honor their founding missions. [economictimes.indiatimes.com](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/topic/us-judges-ai-policies)
- **DOJ backs xAI lawsuit to kill Colorado's AI Act.** The Department of Justice intervened on April 24 in xAI's lawsuit challenging Colorado's sweeping AI Act, marking the first time the federal government has moved to invalidate a state AI law under Trump's Executive Order 14365. The DOJ argued the law violates the Equal Protection Clause by compelling AI companies to factor demographic characteristics into model design, framing state-level AI regulation as a barrier to national competitiveness. [governmentcontractorcomplianceupdate.com](https://www.governmentcontractorcomplianceupdate.com/2026/04/28/federal-government-intervenes-in-case-seeking-to-invalidate-colorado-ai-law/)
- **China blocks Meta's $2B acquisition of Manus.** Beijing formally blocked Meta's $2 billion bid to acquire Manus, an AI agent startup, following a months-long regulatory probe. The decision underscores how cross-border AI acquisitions are increasingly caught in geopolitical crossfire, with data sovereignty and AI agent capabilities now treated as strategic national interests. [cnbc.com](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/27/meta-manus-china-blocks-acquisition-ai-startup.html)
- **OpenAI ends Microsoft exclusivity, expands to AWS.** OpenAI has resolved its long-running legal and commercial tensions with Microsoft, clearing the way to offer its models on Amazon Web Services and potentially other cloud providers. The deal marks a significant shift from the exclusive Azure partnership that defined OpenAI's commercial infrastructure for years. [reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/microsoft-end-exclusive-license-openais-technology-2026-04-27/)
- **AI's regulatory honeymoon is officially over in 2026.** A wave of major AI regulations is hitting simultaneously: the EU AI Act's high-risk provisions take full effect in August with penalties up to €35 million, Colorado's AI Act is due June 30, California's AI Transparency Act kicks in by August, and a 42-state attorney general coalition is ramping up coordinated enforcement. Companies that treated compliance as optional are now facing a hard reckoning. [kavout.com](https://www.kavout.com/market-lens/is-the-ai-regulatory-honeymoon-truly-over)
