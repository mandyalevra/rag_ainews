# AI Daily Digest — May 10, 2026

**TL;DR:** OpenAI's ChatGPT spreadsheet integration is now live for nearly all plan tiers, letting users build and edit Excel and Google Sheets files with natural language directly in a sidebar. · Ramp is in talks to hit a $40B+ valuation just six months after its $32B round, while Kalshi doubled its valuation to $22B in five months. · Tech lobbying has hit $340M in Q1 2026 alone as OpenAI, Meta, and Coinbase race to shape AI legislation before the midterms reshape Congress.

## 🔧 AI Tool & Product News
- **ChatGPT moves into your spreadsheet.** OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets, a sidebar-native AI experience available to nearly all plan tiers — from Free to Enterprise. It can build spreadsheets from scratch, explain formulas, and make edits via natural language. Business and Enterprise customers get a free preview through June 2, 2026, after which usage counts against plan credits. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/zh-hant-hk/articles/20001063-%E6%B8%AC%E8%A9%A6%E7%89%88-chatgpt-for-excel-and-google-sheets)
- **GPT-5.4 mini rolls out as a fallback model.** OpenAI has deployed GPT-5.4 mini inside ChatGPT, but it won't appear as a selectable option in the model picker. Instead, it acts as a fallback for Plus, Pro, and paid users who hit their GPT-5.4 Thinking usage limits, keeping reasoning capabilities available during heavy use sessions. Free and Go users can access it via the 'Thinking' menu toggle. [help.openai.com](https://help.openai.com/pl-pl/articles/9624314-model-release-notes)
- **FLUX 2 arrives with 8-reference character consistency.** Black Forest Labs' FLUX 2 Max model now accepts up to eight reference images and maintains character consistency across image batches, making it useful for product photography, e-commerce, and cinematic keyframe generation. A lighter sibling, FLUX 2 Klein 4B, supports up to five references and generates images faster. Both are available via Replicate's API. [replicate.com](https://replicate.com/collections/flux)
- **Anthropic's Mythos finds bugs in Firefox.** Anthropic's ultra-powerful Mythos AI model was used by Mozilla to identify high-severity security bugs in Firefox, marking one of the first high-profile cases of a frontier model being applied directly to open-source software vulnerability discovery. The news follows Mythos's debut, which prompted the federal CIO to convene discussions about releasing a modified government version. [coaio.com](https://coaio.com/news/2026/05/breaking-tech-news-on-may-8-2026-ai-innovations-space-races-and-2pcc/)

## 🌐 General AI News
- **$340M: tech's Q1 lobbying splurge.** Technology and telecom interests spent more than $340 million on federal lobbying in just the first quarter of 2026 — more than the entire 2022 midterm cycle. OpenAI, Meta, and Coinbase have each more than doubled their in-house lobbying staff since January, with a new industry group called the American Innovation Compact running TV ads framing AI oversight as a threat to U.S. competitiveness. [thechroniclepage.com](https://thechroniclepage.com/article/tech-lobbying-surge-reshapes-policy-agenda-ahead-of-2026-midterms)
- **Congress closes in on a national AI framework.** Reps. Lieu and Obernolte introduced the American Leadership in Artificial Intelligence Act, a consolidated bipartisan bill that pulls together provisions from prior legislation and the 2024 AI Task Force report. Separately, a White House official signaled states may retain some AI regulatory authority — particularly on child safety and government procurement — while a national baseline takes shape. [nextgov.com](https://www.nextgov.com/topic/regulations/more/?page=1)
- **Pentagon breaks up with single-vendor AI.** The Pentagon's Under Secretary for Research and Engineering declared the Defense Department will 'never again' rely on a single AI provider, announcing new flexible contracts with multiple Big Tech firms as a direct counterstatement to its ongoing conflict with Anthropic. The Commerce Department's AI safety center is simultaneously opening classified model evaluations to Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI. [nextgov.com](https://www.nextgov.com/artificial-intelligence/)
- **AI regulation: when, not if.** A new analysis finds that 1,561 AI-related bills have been introduced in U.S. states so far in 2026 — a record — even as federal legislation stalls across fragmented committee jurisdictions. Polling shows 68% of voters now call tech regulation a top priority, up from 41% in 2024, and more Americans worry federal AI rules won't go far enough than worry they'll go too far. [brucemehlman.substack.com](https://brucemehlman.substack.com/p/six-chart-sunday-ai-regulations-are)
- **GUARD Act sparks First Amendment fight.** The Senate Judiciary Committee's passage of the GUARD Act — targeting AI chatbots with age verification, content mandates, and criminal penalties up to $100,000 per violation — has ignited a First Amendment debate. Critics argue the bill forces companies to suppress constitutionally protected speech and compels government-scripted disclosures in every user interaction, a model they say Congress cannot constitutionally impose. [theblaze.com](https://www.theblaze.com/columns/opinion/overzealous-ai-regulation-is-a-danger-to-free-speech)
